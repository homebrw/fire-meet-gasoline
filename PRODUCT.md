# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Co-parents and blended families managing complex custody schedules and shared events. Users operate in high-stakes contexts where miscommunication can trigger conflict or disrupt children's routines. They need clarity, trust, and a system that reduces friction in shared planning.

## Product Purpose

**Famille Sync** coordinates custody schedules, event planning, and transitions (pickups/dropoffs) for co-parenting families. It surfaces each person's availability, custody periods, and upcoming transitions in a unified calendar view so all adults stay aligned and nothing is missed. Success is achieved when the interface reduces scheduling ambiguity and conflict, and acts as a single source of truth.

## Brand Personality

**Clear, calm, reliable.** The interface prioritizes legibility and confidence. Information hierarchy is tight; visual noise is eliminated. Users should feel that the system is trustworthy and understands the weight of what they're coordinating. No cuteness, no excess decoration—just calm competence.

## Anti-references

- **Overly cute or playful** (gamification, mascots, childish tone) — this is serious family logistics, not entertainment
- **Too many bright colors or chaotic visual density** — overwhelm obscures clarity; focus should be effortless
- **Cutesy family imagery or stock photos** — generic clichés undermine trust and accessibility; use data and semantic color instead

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity first** — Information hierarchy and contrast are non-negotiable. Every action, status, and date is legible.
2. **Reduce cognitive load** — One view, one job. Settings are grouped; calendars are scannable.
3. **Trust through consistency** — Colors carry meaning (person, availability, transition type); patterns are predictable so users develop mental models quickly.
4. **Accessibility is not optional** — WCAG AA contrast and motion respect are table stakes. Multilingual support is planned.
5. **Data-driven, not decorative** — Visual elements serve information, not aesthetics. Color, size, and position mean something.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG AA standard** minimum; all text ≥4.5:1 contrast (body) and ≥3:1 (large text)
- **Reduced motion support** — respect `prefers-reduced-motion`; transitions are present but optional
- **Internationalization ready** — currently French; structure allows other languages
- **Color is not the only signal** — availability and status are signaled via text labels and icons, not color alone
